Project: THE Y FILES

Concept & Creative Direction

The project explores the contrast between serious investigative tone and insignificant subject matter. Episodes follow a consistent structure: Ominous, controlled setup Evidence-driven forensic analysis Absurd but inevitable conclusion Deadpan closing statement The writing emphasizes: Behavioral analysis framed as forensic evidence Clear violations and accountability Clinical, objective tone without comedic delivery cues Example cases include missing food, thermostat changes, and interrupted television viewing, all treated with the gravity of formal investigations .

Audio & Voice Design

The narration is produced using AI voice synthesis, designed to replicate the cadence, pacing, and tonal characteristics of classic true-crime documentary narrators. Key considerations: Controlled pacing and intentional pauses to create tension Low-dynamic delivery to maintain a documentary feel Subtle background scoring to support, not overpower, narration Silence used as a structural tool for emphasis The result is an audio experience that feels authentic to the genre while delivering contrast-driven humor.

Technical Implementation

Scriptwriting and narrative structure designed for 60–90 second formats Voice generated using AI text-to-speech / voice cloning tools Audio post-processing includes: Timing refinement Background music layering Level balancing and clarity optimization Final output formatted for social media distribution (Reels, Shorts, etc.) using static or minimal visual assets

Branding & Presentation

The project is presented as a serialized collection of "case files," supported by: Minimal, modern visual identity (logo + banner) Consistent episode titling (e.g., Crumbs of Evidence, Degrees of Deception) Standalone episodes that build a cohesive series over time

Outcome & Purpose

This project demonstrates: Ability to blend creative writing with structured narrative systems Use of AI tools for voice synthesis and content production Understanding of tone, pacing, and audience perception in audio media Development of a scalable, repeatable content format
